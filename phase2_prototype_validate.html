Phase 2

Prototype + Validate

Test the model with a small group of real clients — timing relative to the full build depends on how capital arrives

Overview

What This Raise Funds

⚠️

Not funded by the current raise. The actual cost of building and running the prototype is estimated at roughly $200k to build, plus an ongoing monthly maintenance cost that hasn't been sized yet. That's separate from the ~$1M total raise this phase's timeframe would need beyond the $500k expected in late Aug / early Sept (which only covers Phase 1 hardening) — most of that ~$1M funds marketing, SG&A, and a deliberate technology/team buffer (see below), not the build itself. That ~$1M raise doesn't have to be its own separate round — it could just as easily be the opening tranche of the same $3–5M raise that eventually funds Phase 3, landing in installments rather than as two different fundraising efforts. These are rough estimates, not a priced plan.

Build

Where the Money Goes

Where the ~$1M estimate goes
  • A working prototype is built using AI-assisted development tools with a small product and engineering team — not a full enterprise build*
  • It's tested with 5–10 selected clients drawn from the larger hardened Phase 1 base — not a separate platform serving everyone, a focused beta group
  • Everyone else keeps using the hardened Phase 1 platform normally while the beta group tests the new prototype in parallel
  • Once built, the prototype has an ongoing monthly maintenance cost that hasn't been sized yet — a real, recurring expense on top of the one-time ~$200k build
  • The ~$800k beyond the prototype build is a separate, deliberate buffer, not yet broken into line items — it's meant to cover marketing, SG&A, and the reality that scaling past a one-person product/technology/operations team requires hiring, plus compute/storage, cybersecurity, and compliance costs that don't show up in a pure dev budget
  • Work produced in the prototype isn't thrown away — it becomes the tested blueprint for the Phase 3 full build, whether that follows sequentially or runs in parallel
Build

What Gets Built

What the Prototype Covers — 3 Feature Areas*

1 — AngelSpan IR platformCore startup investor relations functionality on a modern foundation — profiles, update submission, PA review and curation, SMART IR scoring, stakeholder management, and billing. Improved account management and security (MFA, multi-user access) that the current platform lacks. Tested with the beta group before any broader rollout.
2 — Legacy Fund integrationThe first digital connection between a startup's AngelSpan data and the Legacy Fund layer. A startup can apply for funding through a portal, be screened for eligibility, and have their SMART IR scoring history inform the Champion review process.
3 — Portfolio managementFund-level views for GPs, LPs, and Champions to monitor portfolio companies, track SMART IR performance, and manage fund activity. Designed to support Legacy Fund operations once capital is allocated — scope confirmed through real usage during the beta.
Decision

Benefits & Trade-offs

Benefits

  • Real customers, GPs, and LPs use it and give feedback before the full build locks in its design
  • The prototype build itself (~$200k) is a small fraction of Phase 3's ~$3-5M raise
  • Nothing here is wasted — the prototype becomes the tested blueprint for the full build either way
  • Because the beta group is a subset of the already-hardened Phase 1 base, it doesn't put the platform serving everyone else at risk

Risks & Trade-offs

  • Building it needs its own ~$1M raise, separate from and not covered by the current $500k — it depends on future fundraising happening at all
  • Built quickly with a small team and AI-assisted tools; not the same rigor as a fully engineered system under real load. Ongoing maintenance after launch is a real cost that hasn't been sized yet
  • The ~$800k beyond the prototype is currently a buffer, not a priced plan — hiring, infrastructure, cybersecurity, and compliance costs are flagged but not yet itemized
  • This is not the AI-generated demo that already exists — that demo has no real backend and can't take on an actual paying client. This prototype would need to be built from scratch.
