Path 2

Prototype + Validate

Prove the model works before committing full capital

Monthly spend rate — 16-month view
  • A working end-to-end prototype is built in 2 months — covering startup investor reporting, fund management tools, and internal staff workflows — functional enough for real use from day one*
  • The prototype also serves as an emergency backup if the existing platform fails — protecting current customers during the transition to a modern system
  • After the build, spend drops to ~$25k/mo — a small team keeps the platform running while early fund operations generate real usage and revenue data
  • At month 6–8, a clear decision point: if the model is working, invest to build the full platform. If not, stop — with far less capital at risk than Path 1
  • Total spend to reach that decision: ~$200k — compared to ~$1M already fully deployed by that same point in Path 1
  • The prototype is built to support improvements to workflows for both customers and internal staff — not just a replica of what exists today

What the Prototype Must Cover — Subject to Engineering Scoping*

Internal staff tools — investor report curation, SMART IR scoring workflow
Payment processing — subscription billing, correcting known issues in current system
Legacy Fund features — fund creation, startup applications, investor and fund manager access
Email and notifications — delivery of reports and updates to stakeholders
Role-based access — staff, startup clients, fund investors, fund managers, and sponsors
Data migration — bringing over existing client history, contacts, and past reports
Security baseline — multi-factor login and multi-user account access
Automated report output — replacing the current manual process for generating investor reports
Infrastructure — decisions on hosting, compute, and data storage
Client onboarding — migration support, user guides, and training for existing customers
