The Investment Thesis

Why This, Why Now

AngelSpan isn't just fixing an aging platform — it's built on a specific, researched argument for why early-stage investing is broken, and what fixes it. This is that argument, and where the raise ties back to it.

Source: Joe Milam, four articles in The Journal of Private Equity (2018–2019) · Distilled July 2026
The Problem

Private Markets Have No GAAP

Public markets work because reporting is standardized and mandated — GAAP, SEC filings, and an entire analytics industry (S&P, Bloomberg, CapIQ) built on top of that standard. Early-stage private investing has none of this. Startups report irregularly, on no common standard, to whoever happens to ask. That gap is what lets information asymmetry get monetized instead of solved — investors and intermediaries can charge for access to information, not just for capital.

The 2013 JOBS Act tried to fix half of this problem — easier access to funding, via crowdfunding and Reg A+. It never touched the other half: standardized reporting from the companies raising that capital.

SMART IR is AngelSpan's answer to the half the JOBS Act left alone — a standardized reporting expectation for startups, in place of the ad hoc updates the rest of the industry still runs on. Standardized information is what let public markets become efficient; nothing equivalent has existed for private, early-stage companies until now.
The Model

Legacy Funds Implements the "Modern Fund"

The traditional venture model is structurally misaligned with LP interests: high fees regardless of performance, concentrated "hunt for unicorns" portfolios, and after-tax returns nobody bothers optimizing. Legacy Funds is a direct, structural response to each of those problems — not a pitch deck reaction to them.

The Advantage

The After-Tax Advantage: 23.8%, for Structuring Correctly

Two tax provisions most VC funds don't bother structuring around: Section 1202 makes gains on qualifying small-business stock — held 5+ years — 100% federal-tax-free up to the first $10M of profit (or 10× cost basis, whichever is higher). Section 1244 makes losses on the same qualifying stock deductible against ordinary income, not just capital gains. Legacy Funds structures every investment to qualify from the start.

Most funds don't educate LPs on this, and most founders don't know to ask for C-Corp structuring that preserves it. Family offices and high-net-worth individuals — the fastest-growing LP segment — care specifically about after-tax returns. Legacy Funds makes QSBS optimization a headline feature, not a footnote.

The Geography

Diversifying Away From Two Zip Codes

Venture capital is heavily concentrated: the large majority of U.S. VC dollars flow to just two regions — California/Oregon/Washington and the New York/New Jersey corridor. Silicon Valley and Boston had roughly six decades to build their startup ecosystems; most of the country hasn't had that runway, and traditional VC portfolio construction doesn't translate well to smaller, less-networked markets.

Legacy Funds' sponsor-created, place-based funds put local capital behind local founders, with local Champions who know the market — reducing the systemic risk of a portfolio concentrated in two or three metro areas, and reaching deal flow traditional VC structurally overlooks. It also aligns with how institutional LPs increasingly evaluate opportunities: pensions, endowments, and family offices are placing growing weight on impact and ESG criteria alongside pure returns.

The Defensibility

Why This Is Defensible

Incumbent VC firms can't easily copy this. Adopting standardized reporting, transparent selection, and QSBS optimization would mean admitting the 2%-and-20%, opaque-selection model they're built on is the problem being solved. The advantage compounds rather than erodes: standardized reporting builds LP trust, trust attracts more capital, more capital onboards more startups, more startups generate more scoring data — and that data (SMART IR history across 100+ companies) becomes a harder-to-replicate asset the longer the platform runs.
