Board & Governance Considerations

Non-technical items outside the engineering roadmap that the board should have visibility into — legal, compliance, governance, and reporting, not build status.

Prepared July 2026

Legal & Compliance Counsel — Not Yet Engaged

Legacy Funds' application process, fund administration, K-1 generation, and QSBS eligibility determination all carry legal and regulatory weight beyond a typical product build. The March 2026 planning document explicitly flagged this as a gate: investment law counsel should review the platform specification before Legacy Funds engineering begins. No counsel has been identified or engaged yet.

Needs a decision: identify and engage investment law counsel before Legacy Funds work begins in earnest — Phase 2's first integration, or Phase 3's full build.

SOC 2 — What It Is, and Why It May Merit Consideration

SOC 2 (System and Organization Controls 2) is an independent audit, under AICPA standards, that verifies a company's security, availability, and confidentiality controls actually work — not just that they're documented. It isn't a legal requirement; it's a trust signal institutional buyers use as a vendor-diligence gate.

Why it may matter here: institutional LPs (pensions, endowments, family offices) and larger fund sponsors often require SOC 2 or an equivalent before allocating capital through, or entrusting financial data to, a third-party platform — and AngelSpan/Legacy Funds handles exactly the kind of data (startup financials, investor holdings, fund administration records) that SOC 2 is built around. Certification requires 12+ months of audit-ready operation and can't be compressed by spending more — only started earlier.

For discussion: the current plan defers formal SOC 2 certification to the $5M scenario / Phase 4. Worth the board weighing in on whether that timeline is acceptable given the institutional LP segment Legacy Funds is targeting, or whether foundational controls (audit logging, access control, incident response — see below) should start earlier even if certification itself stays deferred.

AI and Client Data — No Current Plans, Flagged for Later

There are no definitive plans today to run client data through AI. If AI-assisted scoring gets pursued later (see the AI strategy on the Implementation Plan page), it would need a data processing agreement with whichever model provider is used, confirming client data isn't used for model training; disclosure in the Terms of Service before the feature is enabled; and sign-off against the six-gate framework already defined for AI features.

No action needed now — flagging this so it's a decision point before any AI feature touches real client data, not an afterthought once one is already built.

Scoring Methodology — Documented, Could Be More Robust

The SMART IR / Bell Mason scoring methodology — formulas, weightings, thresholds, stage-classification logic — is documented and accessible to AngelSpan personnel today, not confined to one person's head. What it isn't yet is formalized to the level a development partner could build against without ambiguity or repeated back-and-forth clarification.

Worth doing before Phase 2 or 3 engineering begins: tighten the existing documentation into a single authoritative specification. Reduces both build risk and the underlying key-person dependency as the team scales.

Cybersecurity Policies — Tied to SOC 2

Separate from technical hardening (Phase 1's engineering work), there are no documented cybersecurity policies today — an incident response plan, an access control policy, a data handling/classification policy, a vendor security review process. These aren't code; they're written organizational commitments. They're also literally what a SOC 2 audit evaluates.

Doesn't require Phase 3 funding to start: drafting these earlier reduces near-term risk and shortens the runway to SOC 2 readiness whenever that gets pursued.

Dev-Partner Contract Structure

Not yet officially decided. Time-and-materials is the current thinking, not fixed-price, but this hasn't been finalized. Worth noting for whenever it is: the March 2026 plan's own risk mitigation for dev-partner scope creep suggested pairing time-and-materials with hard budget caps per phase — a lightweight companion practice worth considering alongside whichever structure gets decided on.

These are governance and compliance considerations, not engineering scope — they don't block Phase 1 and most don't block Phase 2. They're listed here so the board can weigh in on timing and ownership before any of them becomes urgent rather than after.

Confidential — prepared for AngelSpan leadership, board, and prospective investors and partners. Not for public distribution.
