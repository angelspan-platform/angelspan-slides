What This Raise Funds

⚠️

Not on the table for this raise. The tech build is estimated at a consistent ~40% of whatever this raise brings in — about $1.2M at a $3M raise, scaling up to about $2.0M at $5M, over roughly 18 months. That's the cost of building the platform itself; the rest funds Legacy Fund operations, staffing at real scale, marketing, and other non-tech costs, not the build. That $3–5M can land in one lump sum or in stages — if it lands in stages, Phase 2's ~$1M can be the opening tranche of this same target rather than separate capital on top of it, with the remainder completing this phase at the same ~40% allocation. The 40% figure is sourced from 2026 tech-raise planning, and applies to capital raised beyond the current $500k bridge — not to the bridge itself, which stays allocated as already shown on the roadmap. The $500k expected in late Aug / early Sept doesn't touch either figure — this is a future decision, not one for today.

Build

Where the Money Goes

Build cost vs. total raise for this phase
  • At full scope, the platform covers startup investor reporting, Legacy Fund management tools, and internal staff operations, built to run at real scale from the start
  • The tech build itself is a consistent ~40% of the raise — about $1.2M at $3M, scaling up to about $2.0M at $5M, over ~18 months; the rest funds the organization standing up around it: Legacy Fund operations, staffing beyond a one-person team, marketing, and SG&A
  • A build at this scope takes many months before it's serving customers at full scale — real market feedback on the finished platform, on its own, would arrive later than either earlier phase
  • If $3–5M arrives directly, we skip a separate funded Phase 2 pilot and instead use the existing working mock throughout the build to gather feedback — a lighter, no-added-cost check, not a substitute for real client usage data
  • If ~$1M arrives first and Phase 2 already ran, this build starts already informed by real beta feedback, lowering (not eliminating) the risk of building the wrong thing
  • Whichever order it happens in, this is the largest financial commitment of the three phases and depends on a substantially larger raise landing
Decision

Benefits & Trade-offs

Benefits

  • Ships as one complete, production-ready platform — nothing deferred, nothing to explain away as "prototype"
  • Every workflow — founder reporting, Legacy Fund management, internal operations — is built to run at real scale from day one
  • No "build it twice" risk: this is the end-state architecture, not a bridge that gets replaced later
  • Strongest possible platform to show a future institutional partner — a finished product, not a work in progress

Risks & Trade-offs

  • Requires the full $3–5M secured, not just the ~$1M of Phase 2 — the largest capital dependency of the three phases, even though the tech build itself is only ~40% of it (~$1.2–2.0M)
  • The other ~60% (~$1.8–3.0M) funds staffing, Legacy Fund operations, marketing and SG&A — real costs, but not part of what "building the platform" actually costs
  • If this raise arrives directly (without Phase 2 running first), feedback comes only from the existing working mock, not real paying clients — a lighter, partial substitute for the validation a funded Phase 2 pilot would provide
  • Not achievable with the ~$125k set aside for tech in the current raise, or with Phase 2's ~$1M — this is a future decision, not one for today
